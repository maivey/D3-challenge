Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty, Age, and Income

Poverty As illustrated above, there is a positive correlation between poverty and healthcare, smoking, and obesity. That is, the percentage of poverty increases with an increase in the percentage of lack of healthcare. There is also a positive correlation between poverty and smoking. An increase in the percentage of poverty corresponds to an increase in the percentage of people who smoke. Finally, there is a positive correlation between poverty and obesity. The states with a higher percentage of poverty tend to have a higher percentage of obesity in those states.

Age There is a negative correlation between age and lack of healthcare. The states with a lower median age tend to have a higher percentage of people who lack healthcare. There is a weak positive linear correlation between age and percentage of smokers in the state. There is a very weak negative linear correlation between age and obesity. Thus, more analysis must be done to determine if age and percentage of smokers and percentage of obesity exists.

Income There is a negative correlation between income and healthcare, smoking, and obesity. The states that have a higher median income tend to have a lower percentage of people who lack healthcare. The states that have a lower median income tend to have a higher percentage of people who smoke. Likewise, the states that have a lower median income tend to have a higher obesity percentage.
